📖 Forest Wildlife Encyclopedia

🦌 Forest Deer

Gentle herbivores that disperse plant seeds throughout the forest floor. Essential for maintaining balanced forest vegetation.

Tip: Protect wildlife corridors from road traffic and deforestation.

🦊 Red Fox

Clever omnivores that control rodent populations. They adapt quickly to varying habitats but suffer from plastic litter contamination.

Tip: Never leave open tin cans or plastic rings in nature reserves.

🦦 River Otter

Playful aquatic mammals that act as key indicators of clean water health. Sensitive to chemical runoff and floating plastic debris.

Tip: Support river cleanup drives and reduce household detergent runoff.

🦉 Great Horned Owl

Silent nocturnal apex predators that nest in old-growth hollow trees. Logging threatens their breeding sanctuaries.

Tip: Preserve dead standing trees (snags) for bird nesting habitats.

🐻 Grizzly Bear

Keystone guardians of the mountain wilderness. By enriching river banks with salmon nutrients, they feed surrounding ancient trees.

Tip: Respect wilderness boundaries and store camping food securely.

🐺 Timber Wolf

Pack hunters that restore river ecosystems by regulating grazing herds. When wolves thrive, riverbanks stabilize and forests flourish.

Tip: Advocate for wildlife conservation parks and endangered species laws.
